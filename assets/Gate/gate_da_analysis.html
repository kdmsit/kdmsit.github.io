  • Machine Learning remains consistently important (~17%)

🎯 Preparation Strategy

🔥Tier 1: Core Focus (Target 40-50 marks)

Time Allocation: 60% of study time

1. Probability & Statistics (20-22 marks potential)

  • Distributions: Poisson, Normal, Exponential, Bernoulli (6-8 marks)
  • Conditional Probability & Bayes Theorem: (4-6 marks)
  • Expectation, Variance, Covariance: (4-6 marks)
  • Central Limit Theorem & Approximations: (2-4 marks)

2. Machine Learning (15-17 marks potential)

  • Classification: SVM, Naive Bayes, k-NN, Logistic Regression (6-8 marks)
  • Clustering: k-means, hierarchical, single/complete linkage (3-4 marks)
  • Neural Networks: Backpropagation, activation functions (3-4 marks)
  • Dimensionality Reduction: PCA, Fisher discriminant (2-3 marks)

3. Linear Algebra (11-15 marks potential)

  • Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors: Properties, computation (4-5 marks)
  • Matrix Operations: Determinants, rank, inverse (3-4 marks)
  • Subspaces & Projections: Basis, orthogonality (2-3 marks)
  • SVD & Decomposition: Singular values (2-3 marks)

⚡Tier 2: Important Topics (Target 20-25 marks)

Time Allocation: 30% of study time

4. Databases (6-12 marks)

  • SQL: Joins, aggregations, nested queries, GROUP BY
  • Relational Algebra: Selection, projection, joins, division
  • Normalization: BCNF, functional dependencies
  • Indexing: B+ trees, hash indices, OLAP operations

5. Calculus & Optimization (9-10 marks)

  • Derivatives & Limits: Chain rule, L'Hôpital's rule
  • Optimization: Local/global minima and maxima
  • Gradient & Directional Derivatives:
  • Continuity & Differentiability:

6. Data Structures & Algorithms (10-20 marks)

  • Trees: Binary, BST, traversals, properties
  • Graphs: DFS, BFS, topological sort, shortest paths
  • Hashing: Collision resolution, probing
  • Sorting: Quicksort, mergesort, time complexity

💡Tier 3: Supplementary Topics (Target 5-10 marks)

Time Allocation: 10% of study time

7. Artificial Intelligence (4-9 marks)

  • Search Algorithms: A*, alpha-beta pruning
  • Heuristics: Admissibility, consistency
  • Game Trees: Minimax, adversarial search
  • Bayesian Networks: Inference, conditional independence

8. Programming & Logic (2-4 marks)

  • Python: Code tracing, data structures
  • Pseudocode: Algorithm analysis
  • Propositional Logic: Tautologies, equivalences

⏰ Study Plan Recommendation

✅90-Day Preparation Plan

  • Days 1-40 (Core Foundation): Focus on Tier 1 topics - Probability, ML, Linear Algebra
  • Days 41-65 (Important Topics): Cover Tier 2 topics - Databases, Calculus, DSA
  • Days 66-75 (Supplementary): Complete Tier 3 topics - AI, Programming
  • Days 76-90 (Revision & Practice): Solve previous papers, mock tests, weak areas

📌Daily Study Routine (4-6 hours)

  • 2-3 hours: Theory and concept building
  • 1-2 hours: Problem solving and numerical practice
